The Journey

The journey that this site is going to take women on once they are not confined by the fence.

  • Fence to Fields and beyond

    My Humble Beginnings

    I am a woman in recovery that has returned to prison four times and feel that if I had someone helping me what it takes to have a solid foundation I wouldnt have returned. I am wanting to help women in the system to know what is needed to be successful and have a solid plan to release into the community!

  • August 2021

    Hope has arrived!

    We as women in recovery on the journey from fence to fields are not alone. I would like woman to know that they can successfully release and stay in recovery. We deserve this chance to be successful and proud!

  • August 2021

    Recidivism as women with Co-Occuring mental and substance Issues

    Women are more likely to suffer from co-occuring issues, like mental illness and addiction. We are a higher risk for recidivism and relapse then men. Women are needing treatment designed for us! So keep in mind when planning to think of having a back up plan. We are aware things happen and there has to be ajustments along the path, sometimes we have to put pieces of the back up plan into effect to make this journey a successful one.

  • August 2021

    Mission Statement

    This is to help reduce recidivism among women who are being incarcerated each year so the statistics will begin to fall not rise. I am here to help accomplish a successful release from prison which is women to have a solid release plan. I want women to come back to help others in the situation they were once in. We as women need to remember we are wives, mothers, daughters, granddaughters, aunts, friends and will become CEO's. The fence is the end of incarceration and the field is the new begining!

Release Checklist

Making sure everything is finalized and your ready to release sucessfuly

Employment, do you have a job set for your release. Housing, what are your plans for housing upon release? Substance, mental health treatments and appointments. Primary care Physician and an appointment, Your Proper Identification(ID or Drivers License, Social Security card), Your transportation to and from work and set appointments. List of Current Medications that will need to be refilled in two weeks after release, Do you have proper clothing and if not someone helping you with that situation, Probation, do you have an appointment set up for 24 hours after release, Cell phone to have communication with probation and all your Physicians, Final thing to check off is your ride thats picking you up the morning of your release!
